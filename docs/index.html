World News Map

Powered by the GDELT Project

Data source

This map uses the GDELT Events 2.0 dataset, which automatically scans news articles from around the world every 15 minutes and extracts political events — who did what to whom, and where. Each dot on the map represents one news article in which a political event was identified.

News coverage bias

GDELT's coverage is not geographically neutral. The dataset is dominated by English-language and Western media — primarily outlets from North America, the UK, and Australia. Regions with less English-language media presence (Central Africa, Central Asia, parts of Latin America) are significantly underrepresented. High event counts for a country may reflect media attention as much as actual activity.

Event categories

Events are classified using the CAMEO coding scheme, a political science framework that only captures actor-to-actor political interactions. Non-political topics (natural disasters, economics, culture) are not covered.

  • Violence & Conflict — armed fighting, assault, mass violence
  • Protest — demonstrations, strikes, civil unrest
  • Threats & Coercion — threats, shows of force, sanctions, coercion
  • Demands & Rejection — demands, disapproval, rejection, investigations
  • Diplomacy & Cooperation — statements, consultations, aid, agreements

Goldstein scale

The Goldstein sub-mode of Political Landscape colors countries by their Goldstein score — a fixed value assigned to each event type ranging from −10 (most destabilizing) to +10 (most cooperative). The map shows each country's weighted average Goldstein score as an initiating actor (Actor 1), reflecting how aggressively or cooperatively that country behaves in its outgoing interactions.

  • > +4 — strongly cooperative
  • +2 to +4 — cooperative
  • 0 to +2 — slightly cooperative
  • −1 to 0 — neutral
  • −3 to −1 — conflictual
  • −6 to −3 — very conflictual
  • < −6 — highly destabilizing

Media tone

The Tone sub-mode colors countries by the average media tone of news articles in which that country appears as the initiating actor. Tone is derived from GDELT's sentiment analysis of the article text, ranging from very negative (red) to very positive (green). Unlike the Goldstein score — which reflects the event type — tone reflects how the article is written. A country can have cooperative event types but very negative media framing, and vice versa.

  • > +2 — positive coverage
  • 0 to +2 — slightly positive
  • −2 to 0 — slightly negative
  • −5 to −2 — negative
  • < −5 — very negative

Other controls

  • Country search — type a country name to fly to it on the map. In Political Landscape mode, this also opens the Goldstein time series for that country.
  • Most Active — shows the top 5 countries for the current view and period. In News Activity mode: highest event count. In Goldstein mode: most conflictual (lowest score). In Tone mode: most negative coverage. Click any entry to fly to that country.
  • Media Origins — shows which countries the news outlets covering the current period's events are from, inferred from domain TLDs (e.g. .de → Germany). Domains with generic TLDs (.com, .org, .net) are grouped as "US / International" as their origin cannot be reliably determined.
