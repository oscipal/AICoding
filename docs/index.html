World News Map

Powered by the GDELT Project

Data source

This map uses the GDELT Events 2.0 dataset, which automatically scans news articles from around the world every 15 minutes and extracts political events — who did what to whom, and where. Each dot on the map represents one news article in which a political event was identified.

News coverage bias

GDELT's coverage is not geographically neutral. The dataset is dominated by English-language and Western media — primarily outlets from North America, the UK, and Australia. Regions with less English-language media presence (Central Africa, Central Asia, parts of Latin America) are significantly underrepresented.

Event categories

Events are classified using the CAMEO coding scheme, a political science framework that only captures actor-to-actor political interactions.

  • Violence & Conflict — armed fighting, assault, mass violence
  • Protest — demonstrations, strikes, civil unrest
  • Threats & Coercion — threats, shows of force, sanctions, coercion
  • Demands & Rejection — demands, disapproval, rejection, investigations
  • Diplomacy & Cooperation — statements, consultations, aid, agreements

Goldstein scale

The Goldstein sub-mode colors countries by their weighted average Goldstein score — ranging from −10 (most destabilizing) to +10 (most cooperative).

  • > +4 — strongly cooperative
  • +2 to +4 — cooperative
  • 0 to +2 — slightly cooperative
  • −1 to 0 — neutral
  • −3 to −1 — conflictual
  • −6 to −3 — very conflictual
  • < −6 — highly destabilizing

Media tone

The Tone sub-mode colors countries by the average media sentiment of articles in which that country appears as the initiating actor.

  • > +2 — positive coverage
  • 0 to +2 — slightly positive
  • −2 to 0 — slightly negative
  • −5 to −2 — negative
  • < −5 — very negative

Other controls

  • Country search — fly to a country; in Political Landscape mode opens its Goldstein time series.
  • Most Active — top 5 countries for the current view and period. Click any to fly there.
  • Media Origins — news outlet countries inferred from domain TLDs.
  • Hybrid mode — shows a heatmap at low zoom levels that transitions to individual clusters as you zoom in.
